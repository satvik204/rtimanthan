About RTI

The Right to Information (RTI) Act, enacted in India in 2005, empowers citizens to request information from public authorities, promoting transparency and accountability in government. Under this act, individuals can seek details about government policies, decisions, and expenditures. Public authorities are legally required to respond to RTI applications within 30 days, with provisions for faster replies in urgent cases. The act excludes certain sensitive information related to national security, defense, and personal data. RTI is a crucial tool in fighting corruption, enhancing public participation in governance, and holding officials accountable. Citizens can file applications online or in writing, and appeals can be made if information is denied or incomplete.

Steps to apply for RTI

The following steps are to be followed to apply for RTI

Identify the Public Authority

Step 1

Determine the department or public authority that holds the information you seek.

2

Draft the Application

Step 2

Write your application clearly, specifying the information required. Include details like your name, contact information, and address. The questions should be concise and specific.

3

Submit the Application

Step 3

Submit the RTI application in person, by post, or online through the designated RTI portal of the relevant government department.
